            <li><strong class="highlight">Provide delayed signals</strong> - Detecting movements after they have already occurred</li>
            <li><strong class="highlight">Be affected by data errors</strong> - Inaccurate or delayed data from exchanges</li>
        </ul>
        <p>You should never rely solely on our signals for trading decisions. Always use multiple sources of information and analysis.</p>

        <h2><i class="fas fa-balance-scale"></i> 5. Leverage and Margin Risk</h2>
        <p>Some trading strategies involve the use of leverage or margin. While leverage can amplify profits, it also amplifies losses. Trading with leverage can result in losses exceeding your initial investment. Key risks include:</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Liquidation risk:</strong> Your positions may be forcibly closed if the market moves against you</li>
            <li><strong>Funding costs:</strong> Holding leveraged positions overnight may incur funding fees</li>
            <li><strong>Volatility risk:</strong> High leverage combined with volatility can lead to rapid liquidation</li>
        </ul>
        <p>The risk management parameters shown in our documentation (e.g., 1-2% risk per trade) are guidelines only. You should adjust based on your own risk tolerance.</p>

        <p>Using online trading platforms and services involves technical and security risks, including:</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Exchange hacks:</strong> Cryptocurrency exchanges have been hacked, resulting in loss of funds</li>
            <li><strong>Phishing attacks:</strong> Scammers may attempt to steal your login credentials</li>
            <li><strong>Technical failures:</strong> Server outages, internet disruptions, or software bugs</li>
            <li><strong>Smart contract risks:</strong> If trading DeFi tokens, smart contract vulnerabilities</li>
        </ul>
        <p>We recommend using hardware wallets for long-term storage and enabling all available security features on exchanges (2FA, whitelisting, etc.).</p>

        <ul>
            <li>Affect the value or legality of cryptocurrencies</li>
            <li>Impact your ability to trade or access exchanges</li>
            <li>Result in taxes, fines, or other penalties</li>

        <p>Some cryptocurrencies or trading pairs may have low liquidity, which can result in:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Difficulty entering or exiting positions at desired prices</li>
            <li>Significant slippage (difference between expected and actual execution price)</li>
            <li>Wider bid-ask spreads, increasing trading costs</li>
        </ul>
        <p>Low liquidity can amplify losses and make it difficult to manage risk effectively.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="legal-section">
        <h2><i class="fas fa-brain"></i> 10. Psychological Risk</h2>
        <p>Trading can be emotionally stressful and may lead to psychological challenges:</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Fear of missing out (FOMO):</strong> Entering trades impulsively</li>
            <li><strong>Panic selling:</strong> Exiting positions at the worst possible time</li>
            <li><strong>Revenge trading:</strong> Trying to recover losses with larger, riskier trades</li>
            <li><strong>Overconfidence:</strong> Taking excessive risks after a string of wins</li>
        </ul>
        <p>Maintain discipline, stick to your trading plan, and never trade when emotionally compromised.</p>
